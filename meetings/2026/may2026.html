The 36-Inch Blind Spot: Why Major AI Models Fail at Spatial Reasoning

We are happy to announce that Doug Drechsel will be our special guest for this month's meeting.

In the rush to build increasingly massive AI models, an invisible architectural blind spot has emerged: spatial reasoning. What happens when you ask the world's most advanced AI engines - from multi-billion-dollar cloud systems to state-of-the-art local Vision-Language Models (VLMs) - to calculate the total height of a simple shelving unit with overlapping sections?

They all fail.

This session explores the fundamental gap between human visual intuition and AI mathematical calculation. Using a straightforward woodworking diagram as a stress test, we will break down why leading models consistently misinterpret basic geometric relationships.
